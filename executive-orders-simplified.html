Executive Orders Simplified is a personal project that automates the collection, parsing, and indexing of all US presidential actions from WhiteHouse.gov into a structured SQL database, then exposes this corpus through an LLM-powered web interface for plain-language querying. Built at the intersection of technical skills and political science, the project democratizes access to legal and governmental documents for non-expert users who would otherwise be shut out by dense legal language.

Motivation

Presidential executive orders, proclamations, and memoranda carry real legal weight — but they are written in legal register that makes them effectively inaccessible to most people. Built in early 2025 amid a high-volume period of presidential actions, this project asked: what if anyone could query the full record of presidential actions in plain English and get a grounded, accurate answer? The project sits at the intersection of government transparency, NLP, and database engineering.

Automated Data Pipeline

The pipeline fully automates the collection of presidential actions from WhiteHouse.gov — scraping, parsing, and indexing documents into a structured SQL database for legal and policy analysis. Comprehensive data validation methods were implemented to handle the inconsistencies endemic to government web data: varying document formats, encoding issues, and incomplete metadata.

Scraping and Parsing

The scraper retrieves presidential action documents and parses them into structured fields — title, date, type, full text, and relevant metadata. Robust error handling was implemented to manage government data inconsistencies, including documents that deviate from standard formats.

Database Schema

Documents are stored in a structured SQL database with standardized categorization for legal research applications. The schema supports both full-text retrieval and structured filtering by type, date range, and subject. A structured quantitative dataset of executive actions was created with standardized categorization enabling systematic legal analysis.

LLM-Powered Web Interface

The web interface allows users to query the database in plain language — asking questions like "what executive orders addressed immigration in the first 90 days?" — and receive grounded answers derived from the actual document corpus. The LLM component bridges government information accessibility, translating between natural language questions and the structured data layer.

Accessibility and Impact

The core motivation was civic accessibility: making the machinery of government legible to people who lack legal training. This connects to Shalini's interest in political science and the democratic value of an informed public. When executive actions proliferate rapidly, tools that help people understand what is being done in their name have genuine civic importance.
