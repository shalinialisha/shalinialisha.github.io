As a Software Engineering Intern at Adobe (May–August 2024), Shalini spearheaded the modernization of Premiere Pro's Timeline UI, working in C++ alongside designers and engineers. The project applied research on how modern UI aesthetics — including rounded corners and softer visual language — reduce cognitive friction and improve how users feel while doing their work. She organized and led weekly cross-functional progress meetings and culminated the internship with a presentation at Adobe MAX 2024. This role led directly to her full-time position at Frame.io (Adobe).

Project Overview

The internship project centered on modernizing the Timeline UI in Adobe Premiere Pro — the central workspace where video editors assemble, cut, and refine their projects. The Timeline is one of the most performance-critical and high-touch components in the application, used continuously throughout an editing session by professionals ranging from indie filmmakers to broadcast engineers.

Design Research and UI Psychology

A key input to the redesign was research on how modern UI design elements affect users psychologically. Studies have shown that softer visual elements — rounded corners, increased whitespace, and consistent visual weight — reduce perceived cognitive load and make interfaces feel safer and more approachable. Rounded corners in particular exploit the brain's natural preference for shapes without sharp edges, which are associated with safety rather than threat. Applied to a professional tool like Premiere Pro's Timeline, these findings meant that a modernized UI could reduce the stress of complex editing workflows and help users feel more in control of their work.

Shalini collaborated closely with designers to translate these principles into concrete C++ implementations — working from Figma specs through to the rendered UI.

Technical Work

The engineering work was done in C++, requiring deep familiarity with Adobe's proprietary UI framework and object state management patterns. The Timeline component maintains complex real-time state across tracks, clips, playback position, selection state, and edit history — all of which had to be preserved correctly through the visual modernization.

Object State Management

Shalini gained expertise in Adobe's proprietary design patterns and object state management to ensure seamless integration of the new UI with Premiere Pro's existing editing engine.

Project Management

Shalini organized and led weekly progress meetings throughout the summer, incorporating feedback from designers, engineers, and product stakeholders. She presented key updates to management, building skills in communicating technical decisions to non-technical audiences — a practice she continued in her full-time role.

Adobe MAX 2024

The project culminated in a presentation at Adobe MAX 2024, Adobe's annual creative conference. Shalini presented the Premiere Pro Timeline Redesign to an audience of engineers, designers, and creative professionals, demonstrating both the design rationale and the engineering implementation.
