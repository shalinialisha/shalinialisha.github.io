Community Involvement

From SHALIpedia, the personal encyclopedia

A thread running through all of Shalini's work — technical and otherwise — is community. She has founded organizations, planned cultural events, mentored students, and built spaces for people who have historically been excluded from STEM. This page documents that work at Vanderbilt and at Adobe. See also Leadership & Professional Affiliations and her role at Frame.io (Adobe).

At Vanderbilt University

During her time at Vanderbilt (2021–2025), Shalini built two communities from scratch, both responding to gaps she personally experienced in belonging and representation.

ColorStack Vanderbilt Chapter

Shalini founded the Vanderbilt chapter of ColorStack, a national organization connecting Black and Latinx students in computer science. The chapter created a space for students of color in CS to find community, access professional development resources, and support each other through a field where they are often underrepresented.

Latinx & Queer Affinity Group

Shalini also founded the Latinx & Queer Affinity Group at Vanderbilt (2022–2025), creating a space specifically for people navigating the intersection of Latinx and LGBTQ+ identity on campus. The group held programming around community, culture, and mutual support.

Research on Equity in STEM

Shalini's community work at Vanderbilt was directly informed by her research at the PRISM Lab, where she studied the barriers and challenges faced by Latinx students in STEM. That research gave her an empirical lens on the very dynamics she was working to change through organizing.

At Adobe — HOLA ERG

At Adobe, Shalini is an active member of HOLA — the Hispanic and Latino Organization for Leadership and Advancement, Adobe's ERG for Hispanic and Latino employees. She helps plan community-oriented events designed to educate the broader Adobe community on Latino and Hispanic history and culture in engaging and meaningful ways.

HOLA's programming reflects a philosophy Shalini has carried from her Vanderbilt work: that community events are most powerful when they combine celebration with education, giving people outside the community genuine insight rather than surface-level exposure.

Mentorship

Through her role as Grading Assistant for CS 1100 at Vanderbilt, Shalini mentored over 200 students in Python fundamentals, with particular attention to students from underrepresented backgrounds encountering programming for the first time.

Philosophy

Shalini's community work is inseparable from her technical and research interests. The same concern that drives her research into algorithmic fairness — that technology systems can reinforce or reduce inequity depending on who builds them — motivates her investment in making STEM communities more inclusive. Building the pipeline matters as much as fixing the algorithms.
