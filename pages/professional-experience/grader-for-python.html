As a Grading Assistant for CS 1100: Programming & Problem Solving at Vanderbilt University (2022–2025), Shalini supported over 200 students in Python and computer science fundamentals over three years. She contributed to pedagogical innovation through data-driven grading and mentorship, combining technical rigor with accessibility for students encountering programming for the first time. This role overlapped with her founding of ColorStack Vanderbilt, reinforcing her commitment to equity in CS education.

Course Overview

CS 1100 is Vanderbilt's introductory programming course in Python, designed for students with no prior coding experience. The course covers variables, control flow, functions, data structures, and algorithmic thinking. The student population is diverse — many students are first-generation college students or come from non-CS majors exploring computational tools for their fields.
