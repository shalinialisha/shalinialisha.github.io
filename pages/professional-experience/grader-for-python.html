From 2022 to 2025, Shalini served as a Grading Assistant for CS 1100, Vanderbilt's introductory Python programming course. Over three years she supported more than 200 students encountering programming for the first time. See also Technical Skills.

Responsibilities

Shalini's responsibilities went beyond grading submissions. She hosted regular office hours where students could work through bugs, ask conceptual questions, and get unstuck on assignments in real time. During class sessions she circulated to help students as they wrote code live, catching misconceptions early and offering explanations tailored to each student's background.

She provided written feedback on assignments designed to help students understand not just what went wrong, but why — building the debugging intuition they would need as they progressed through the CS curriculum.

Course Overview

CS 1100 is Vanderbilt's gateway programming course, taught in Python. It introduces variables, control flow, functions, data structures, and basic algorithms to students across all majors — many of whom have no prior programming experience. The course is foundational to Vanderbilt's CS curriculum.
