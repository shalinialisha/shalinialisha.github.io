Shalini interned on the Premiere Pro engineering team at Adobe in Summer 2024, where she worked in C++ on the Timeline UI — one of Premiere Pro's most heavily used interfaces. Her primary contribution was modernizing the Timeline with rounded corners, a visual update that shipped to beta users and was presented at Adobe MAX 2024. See also Technical Skills.

Timeline UI Redesign

The Timeline is a core component of Premiere Pro — the primary workspace where editors arrange and cut video clips. Shalini's internship project focused on modernizing the Timeline's visual design, implementing rounded corners across timeline elements as part of a broader UI refresh.

The feature shipped to beta users during the internship period. The public beta discussion can be found on the Adobe Community forum.

Engineering in C++

Premiere Pro is a large-scale native application built in C++. Working in the codebase required navigating a complex, performance-sensitive UI framework with significant legacy infrastructure. This internship deepened Shalini's experience with systems-level programming and large C++ codebases.

Presentation at Adobe MAX 2024

Shalini presented her Timeline UI work at Adobe MAX 2024, Adobe's annual creative conference. The presentation gave her experience communicating technical engineering contributions to a mixed audience of designers, creators, and engineers.
