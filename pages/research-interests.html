Shalini's research interests sit at the intersection of artificial intelligence, ethics, and social justice, with a focus on algorithmic fairness, bias detection, and the impact of AI systems on marginalized communities. She is drawn to mixed-methods approaches that combine computational analysis with qualitative understanding of social contexts, and seeks to develop expertise in responsible AI development and evaluation methodologies. Her work with technical skills across research and industry forms the empirical foundation for these interests.

Deradicalizing Algorithms

Through her research experience and projects, Shalini has clarified a core research focus she calls "deradicalizing algorithms." This refers to the challenge of developing algorithmic systems that maintain user engagement without exploiting psychological vulnerabilities or pushing people toward extremist content.

The Core Problem

Contemporary recommendation systems optimize for engagement metrics. Research has shown that this optimization can drive users toward increasingly extreme content — a phenomenon sometimes called algorithmic radicalization. The technical challenge is designing systems that honor genuine user interests while refusing to weaponize the psychology of radicalization for retention.

Beyond Social Media

While this problem is most visible in social media recommendation, Shalini's interest extends to surveillance systems used in prisons and at borders — spaces where algorithmic bias carries profound consequences for marginalized communities. The stakes of getting these systems wrong are not abstract.
