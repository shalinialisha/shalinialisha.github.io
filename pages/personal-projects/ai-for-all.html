AI for All fine-tuned GPT-2 on a curated corpus of academic scholarship focused on equity — critical race studies, feminist theory, and postcolonial studies — to explore whether a language model trained on this literature would generate text that reflects its values and frameworks. The project won 2nd place at the Vanderbilt DSI AI Symposium in 2023. Code is available on GitHub. See also Literature, Political Science, and Technical Skills.

Motivation

Most large language models are trained on internet-scale data that over-represents certain perspectives and under-represents others. AI for All asked a pointed question: what if the training corpus was deliberately curated around scholarship that centers equity, justice, and the experiences of marginalized communities? Would a model trained on that corpus generate text that reflects those frameworks? The project was an exploration of how training data shapes a model's implicit worldview.

Dataset

The training corpus was assembled from academic works in critical race studies, feminist theory, and postcolonial studies — scholarship that analyzes systems of power, structural inequity, and the politics of representation. Shalini drew on her English minor at Vanderbilt and her engagement with critical theory to select and curate the texts. See the Literature page for more on the scholarly tradition the corpus draws from.

Model and Training

The project fine-tuned GPT-2 on the equity scholarship corpus using standard fine-tuning techniques. Fine-tuning a pre-trained model on a domain-specific corpus allows the model to adapt its outputs toward the vocabulary, tone, and frameworks of that domain without training from scratch. The full code and training pipeline are available on GitHub.

Results and Recognition

The project won 2nd place at the Vanderbilt Data Science Institute AI Symposium in 2023. The evaluation centered on the quality and coherence of the generated text and the originality of the project's framing. See Fellowships and Awards for the full record.

Connection to Research Interests

AI for All connects directly to Shalini's broader research interests in algorithmic fairness. The project is an empirical demonstration of a core fairness question: training data is not neutral, and the choices made in assembling a corpus have downstream consequences for what a model can and cannot say, whose perspectives it reflects, and whose it erases. It also draws on her interest in political science — specifically, how power dynamics and structural inequity show up in the way knowledge is produced and distributed.
