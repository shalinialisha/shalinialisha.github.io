AI for All is an independent research project exploring how NLP can surface underrepresented scholarly perspectives on systemic inequity. Shalini curated a custom dataset of academic literature from scholars analyzing power dynamics and marginalization, fine-tuned GPT-2 on this corpus, and developed an approach for constructive AI-generated dialogue that spotlights excluded voices. The project draws on literature and political science, demonstrates advanced technical skills, and won 2nd place at the 2023 Vanderbilt DSI AI Symposium.

Motivation

Mainstream language models are trained predominantly on text that reflects dominant cultural perspectives — marginalizing, erasing, or distorting the voices of communities that have historically been excluded from canonical discourse. AI for All asks: what would a language model look like if it were trained to center the scholarship of those communities? Could AI be used not to replicate existing inequities but to raise consciousness about them?

Dataset Curation

The dataset was curated from literature by scholars analyzing power dynamics in society, examining systemic inequity and barriers faced by marginalized communities. This required both subject-matter judgment — selecting texts that genuinely represented excluded perspectives — and technical rigor in structuring the corpus for machine learning.

Source Literature

The corpus draws from scholarship in critical race theory, feminist theory, postcolonial studies, and related fields.

Data Validation and Metadata

Comprehensive data validation methods were implemented to ensure dataset quality and representativeness. Quantitative datasets from diverse textual sources were managed and structured with standardized metadata and categorization systems — treating the corpus as a research artifact in its own right.

Model Fine-Tuning

GPT-2 was fine-tuned on the curated corpus using a Python script with Hugging Face Transformers. The fine-tuning process adapted GPT-2's language modeling objective to the specific vocabulary, concepts, and argumentative patterns of the selected scholarship.

The goal was not simply to produce outputs that sounded like the source texts, but to develop a constructive approach for AI to spotlight excluded voices and raise consciousness — through respectful, grounded elucidation of racial, gender, and socioeconomic challenges rather than reductive imitation.

Results and Symposium

The project was presented at the 2023 Vanderbilt Data Science Institute AI Symposium, where it won 2nd place, recognizing excellence in AI-related research. The presentation demonstrated both the technical fine-tuning work and the broader interdisciplinary framing of the project. The same symposium also recognized Shalini's work on the Future of Free Speech Project as a finalist.

Ethical Considerations

Building a model meant to speak in the register of marginalized scholarship raises genuine ethical questions: Who has authority to curate such a corpus? How should a model avoid ventriloquizing the communities it aims to represent? These questions informed the design of the project and connect directly to Shalini's research interests in algorithmic fairness and responsible AI.
