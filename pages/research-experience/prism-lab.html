The PRISM Lab at Vanderbilt University studies equity in STEM education, with a particular focus on the experiences of underrepresented students. As a Research Assistant, Shalini conducted mixed-methods analysis on Latinx STEM student outcomes — combining survey data analysis with qualitative coding of semi-structured interview transcripts to identify barriers and challenges faced by underrepresented students. The work contributed to academic manuscripts, conference presentations, and data visualizations for both research and policy audiences. This role is deeply connected to her community involvement and her use of technical skills for social impact.

About the Lab

Research Focus

The lab's research centers on the educational outcomes of Latinx students in STEM fields — examining why representation gaps persist and what structural, cultural, and institutional factors shape students' experiences and trajectories. The work operates at the intersection of education research, sociology, and data science.

Mixed-Methods Approach

A central feature of the lab's methodology is its integration of quantitative and qualitative approaches — treating them as complementary rather than competing ways of knowing. Shalini developed proficiency in both dimensions of this work.

Quantitative Analysis

Survey data was analyzed to identify patterns in student outcomes, barriers to persistence, and resource utilization. Analysis was conducted using R, with data visualizations and summary tables created for research dissemination.

Qualitative Coding

Semi-structured interview transcripts were coded to identify key themes related to the barriers and challenges faced by underrepresented students. Qualitative coding required close reading, iterative category development, and attention to the nuance of individual experience within structural patterns.

Publications and Presentations

Shalini collaborated with the research team to draft reports and manuscripts for academic and policy audiences, and created visualizations for conference presentations.

Connection to Community Work

The research at PRISM Lab provided an empirical foundation for Shalini's community organizing work. Understanding the structural barriers Latinx students face in STEM — documented through data — informed how she built the ColorStack Vanderbilt chapter and the Latinx & Queer Affinity Group. Research and community organizing were mutually reinforcing activities throughout her time at Vanderbilt.
