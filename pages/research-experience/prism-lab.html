The PRISM Lab at Vanderbilt studies the experiences and outcomes of Latinx students in STEM fields. Shalini joined as a research assistant, contributing to survey design, data collection, qualitative coding, and data visualization. The lab's work directly informed her community-building efforts at Vanderbilt — she was studying the barriers she was simultaneously working to dismantle. See also Community Involvement and Technical Skills.

Research Focus

The lab uses mixed-methods research to understand what shapes the persistence and success of Latinx students in STEM — including academic, social, and institutional factors. Research questions address belonging, mentorship, institutional support structures, and the cumulative effects of underrepresentation on student trajectories.

Survey Design and Data Collection

Shalini contributed to the design and administration of surveys used to collect data from Latinx STEM students at Vanderbilt and peer institutions. This involved crafting questions that captured both quantitative outcomes and qualitative experiences, and coordinating data collection across multiple participant cohorts.

Qualitative Coding

A significant portion of the research involved qualitative coding — systematically categorizing open-ended survey responses and interview transcripts to identify themes. Shalini applied and developed codebooks, working within a team to ensure intercoder reliability and build a shared analytical framework.

Data Visualization

Shalini built visualizations in R using ggplot2 to represent both quantitative survey results and patterns emerging from qualitative analysis. These visualizations were used to communicate findings to collaborators and stakeholders.

Connection to Community Work

The PRISM Lab research gave empirical grounding to Shalini's community-building work. The barriers she was studying — isolation, lack of belonging, insufficient institutional support — were the same ones she was working to address through ColorStack Vanderbilt and the Latinx & Queer Affinity Group. Research and organizing, in her experience, reinforced each other.
