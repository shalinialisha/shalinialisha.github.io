The Ultimate Consequences Project is a human rights data analysis initiative that documents and visualizes conflict-related deaths in Bolivia. Shalini contributed analysis and visualization work using R, building interactive charts with Crosstalk and Plotly to make the data accessible to policy advocates and researchers. See also Political Science and Technical Skills.

Project Background

The Ultimate Consequences Project collects and analyzes records of deaths resulting from political conflict in Bolivia, with the aim of supporting human rights documentation and policy reform. The project involves systematic data collection, verification, and visualization to produce a public record that can be used by advocates, journalists, and policymakers.

The full dataset and site are available on GitHub.

Data Analysis

Shalini analyzed over 1,000 records of conflict-related deaths using R. The analysis involved data cleaning and validation to ensure records were consistent and usable, followed by statistical summaries and pattern analysis across time periods, regions, and conflict types.

Visualization

A key output of the project is interactive data visualization. Shalini built visualizations using Plotly and Crosstalk in R — tools that allow users to filter and explore the data directly in the browser. The visualizations were designed to make complex datasets legible to non-technical audiences, including human rights advocates and journalists.

Policy Relevance

The project's outputs are intended to inform human rights policy reform in Bolivia. By building a transparent, accessible record of conflict deaths, the project supports accountability and gives advocates a concrete evidentiary basis for policy arguments. This reflects the broader intersection of Shalini's technical work and political science interests.
