The ABC Musicians Project connects hundreds of Nashville musicians to financial resources — grants, emergency funds, and government funding programs — through an interactive R Shiny application. Shalini built data visualizations and contributed analysis to help communicate the landscape of available funding to musicians and advocates. The live app is at abcnashville.shinyapps.io/abc-musicians.

Project Background

Nashville's music economy depends on thousands of independent musicians who often lack access to the financial infrastructure available to workers in more traditional industries. The ABC Musicians Project was designed to address that gap — aggregating and presenting information about financial resources in a way that working musicians could actually navigate.

Data and Visualization

Shalini built visualizations in R to represent the distribution and availability of financial resources, including government funding allocations and private grant programs. The visualizations were designed to make complex funding data legible to musicians and community organizations rather than just policy researchers.

Shiny Application

The project is deployed as an interactive R Shiny application, allowing users to filter and explore available resources by type, eligibility, and deadline. The app is publicly accessible at abcnashville.shinyapps.io/abc-musicians and is maintained as a live resource for the Nashville musician community.

Government Funding Analysis

A component of the project focused specifically on analyzing and visualizing government funding streams available to musicians — including federal, state, and municipal programs. This analysis helped identify gaps in coverage and supported advocacy efforts for expanded public support for Nashville's music workforce.
