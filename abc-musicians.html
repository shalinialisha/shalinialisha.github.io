The ABC Musicians Project was a community-oriented data research project run through the Vanderbilt Data Science Institute, aimed at connecting hundreds of Nashville musicians and artists to financial resources. As a Project Assistant, Shalini conducted surveys, analyzed funding allocation data, and used R to build visualizations that contributed to a successful government funding presentation. The funding was secured.

Background

Nashville's music industry is home to thousands of independent artists and musicians — many of whom lack access to institutional financial support. The ABC Musicians Project was designed to bridge that gap systematically: surveying working musicians to understand their needs and mapping them to available funding opportunities. The project was embedded within the Vanderbilt Data Science Institute, combining rigorous data methodology with direct community impact.

Survey and Data Collection

Shalini conducted surveys connecting hundreds of Nashville musicians and artists to financial resources, gathering data on their needs, income structures, and existing relationships with funding bodies. The survey data was then analyzed to inform funding allocation decisions — determining which resources were most relevant to which segments of the musician community.

Data Analysis and Visualization

Using R, Shalini built programs to analyze the survey data and generate visualizations for the funding presentation. Project management and version control were handled through GitHub. The visualizations translated raw survey responses into clear patterns — showing geographic distribution of need, types of resources most in demand, and gaps in existing coverage.

Government Funding Outcome

The data analysis and visualizations were presented to government stakeholders as part of a funding application. The funding was successfully secured, directly benefiting the Nashville musician community that the project surveyed. This project stands as an example of data science applied to tangible community outcomes — a theme that runs throughout Shalini's work at the Ultimate Consequences Project and beyond.
